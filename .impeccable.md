## Design Context

### Users
Arabic-speaking shoppers in Iraq who are trying to find trustworthy electronics shops, products, and official dealers before they leave home or while moving between markets. They need fast orientation on mobile: which shop is relevant, where it is, whether it is trustworthy, and how to contact it.

### Brand Personality
Street-smart, confident, and precise. The interface should feel local to Iraq, practical for real shopping decisions, and refined enough to build trust without looking corporate or generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial market atlas. Light theme, warm paper-like surfaces, deep ink navigation, calibrated brass and rust accents, large typographic hierarchy, and photography used as the main visual anchor. Avoid generic SaaS cards, neon glows, purple gradients, and dashboard clutter.

### Design Principles
1. Start from destination: every major section should help the user reach a shop, product, street, or city faster.
2. Trust should be visible at a glance through verification, ratings, dealer status, and freshness cues.
3. Mobile scanning comes first: bold hierarchy, restrained copy, and obvious actions.
4. Use structure instead of ornament: layout, type, contrast, and photography do most of the design work.
5. Keep the logic intact while making the public experience feel intentionally rebuilt, not restyled.
